What is a Ponzi Scheme?

A Ponzi scheme is a fraudulent investment operation where returns for existing investors are generated using new investors' money rather than from legitimate business operations. Named after Charles Ponzi, these Ponzi scams inevitably collapse when new investor recruitment slows down.

Common Signs of a Ponzi Scheme:

  • üî∏ Guaranteed high returns with little risk
  • üî∏ Consistent returns regardless of market conditions
  • üî∏ Secretive or complex investment strategies
  • üî∏ Difficulty receiving payments or withdrawing funds
  • üî∏ Pressure to recruit new investors
  • üî∏ Unlicensed sellers or unregistered investments
  • üî∏ Paperwork errors or inconsistencies
  • üî∏ Emphasis on recruiting rather than selling products
